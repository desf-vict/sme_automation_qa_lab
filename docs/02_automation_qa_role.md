# 02 — Automation QA Role

## Purpose

This document explains the role demonstrated by this repository.

The role is not to replace developers.

The role is to support a small developer team by checking whether automations are reliable enough to be delivered to customers.

## Main Responsibilities

An automation QA support role may involve:

- testing developer-built scripts
- checking input and output correctness
- validating customer data handling
- checking API and integration behaviour
- reviewing error messages
- reproducing defects
- documenting bugs clearly
- checking whether the automation works in the customer environment
- confirming whether handover material is usable

## AI-Assisted Automation Responsibilities

When AI is part of the automation, QA should also check:

- whether AI output is valid JSON, CSV, SQL, or text
- whether the AI invents unsupported data
- whether the AI changes schema between runs
- whether human review is required
- whether unsafe assumptions are made
- whether prompts are reliable enough for the workflow

## Practical Positioning

A clear interview explanation would be:

> I focus on validating whether automations, scripts, integrations, and AI-assisted workflows behave correctly and safely before they are delivered to customers.

## Expected Learning Outcome

The reader should understand automation QA as a practical delivery-support discipline.
